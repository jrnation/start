The 42nd Amendment Act (1976)

The "Mini Constitution" passed during the Emergency.

An Act to amend the Constitution of India, enacting widespread changes to the Preamble, Fundamental Duties, and the balance of power between the Judiciary and the Legislature.

In Simple English

Passed during the National Emergency by the Indira Gandhi government, the 42nd Amendment was the most comprehensive amendment in Indian history. It brought about so many changes that it is often called the "Mini Constitution."

The primary goal of this amendment was to assert the supremacy of the Parliament (the legislature) over the Supreme Court (the judiciary) and to centralize power in the hands of the executive.

Key Changes Made:

  • The Preamble: Added three new words: "Socialist", "Secular", and "Integrity".
  • Fundamental Duties: Inserted Part IVA (Article 51A) prescribing 10 fundamental duties for citizens.
  • Judicial Review curtailed: Attempted to strip the Supreme Court of its power to review constitutional amendments.
  • Directive Principles: Made Directive Principles of State Policy (DPSP) superior to Fundamental Rights in certain cases.

Many of the authoritarian changes brought by the 42nd Amendment were later reversed by the Morarji Desai government through the 44th Amendment Act (1978), which restored the balance of power and judicial independence.
