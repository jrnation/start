The Power of Primary Sources

Reading summarized notes is helpful, but the examiner drafts questions based on the exact phrasing of the original law. Ambiguity in your preparation arises when you rely entirely on third-party interpretations.

Our Bare Acts library provides the unadulterated text of critical statutes—such as the Representation of the People Act, the Environment Protection Act, and the Right to Information Act—paired immediately with plain-English annotations to remove the friction of legalese.
